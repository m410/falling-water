Micro-hydro system design

  1. Upstream Water Source

    The natural water source feeding your system. This could be a stream, creek, or river with consistent year-round flow. The ideal source has a steep gradient and reliable water volume, even during dry seasons.

  2. Stream Monitor

    Sensors placed upstream to measure critical water conditions in real-time:

    • Flow rate - Volume of water passing per second (liters/sec or gallons/min)
    • Water depth - Indicates available water volume
    • Temperature - Affects water density and equipment performance
    • Debris detection - Alerts when blockages may occur
  3. Weir and Intake

    A low dam or barrier built across the stream to raise the water level and direct flow into the intake. The weir creates a controlled diversion point while allowing excess water to overflow naturally. A Coanda screen at the intake filters debris and sediment before water enters the penstock.

  4. Penstock

    The enclosed pipe that carries water from the weir down to the generator. Penstock diameter and material affect system efficiency - larger pipes reduce friction losses. The vertical drop (head) between the intake and generator determines the water pressure and potential power output.

  5. Environmental Bypass

    A portion of the stream must always flow freely past the weir to maintain the downstream ecosystem. Environmental regulations typically require a minimum bypass flow to protect aquatic habitats, fish migration, and downstream water users.

  6. Generator / Turbine

    The heart of the system where water pressure is converted to rotational energy, then electricity. The turbine housing protects components and directs water flow optimally.

    • Pelton wheel - Best for high head (30m+), low flow sites. Uses cup-shaped buckets that capture energy from high-pressure water jets.
    • Turgo wheel - Handles higher flow rates than Pelton. Water strikes buckets at an angle, allowing faster rotation and more compact design.
    • Crossflow turbine - Suitable for medium head, higher flow. Water passes through the runner twice, self-cleaning debris.
  7. Power Conditioning

    Converts raw generator output into stable, usable electricity:

    • Rectifier - Converts AC from generator to DC
    • MPPT Controller - Optimizes power extraction as water conditions vary
    • Inverter - Converts DC to household AC (120V/240V)
    • Battery storage - Stores excess energy for peak demand or low-water periods
